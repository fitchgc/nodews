What is Christmas?

A simple guide for English learners

1. The Basics

Christmas is a famous holiday. People celebrate it every year on December 25th. For Christians, it is a religious day to celebrate the birth of Jesus Christ. Today, many people all over the world celebrate it as a time for family and kindness.

2. Common Traditions

  • Christmas Trees: Families put a pine tree in their house and decorate it with lights and ornaments.
  • Giving Gifts: People buy presents for their friends and family to show they care.
  • Santa Claus: Children believe a kind man in a red suit brings toys on Christmas Eve (December 24th).
  • Christmas Carols: These are special songs people sing during the holiday season.

3. Vocabulary to Know

Merry Christmas: The standard way to say "hello" or "goodbye" on this holiday.

Eve: The night before a holiday (December 24th).

Feast: A very large, delicious meal with many people.

Decorations: Beautiful things like lights and stars used to make a place look festive.

4. How to Celebrate

You don't need to be religious to enjoy Christmas! You can celebrate by:

  1. Eating a big dinner with friends.
  2. Watching a Christmas movie.
  3. Wishing people a "Merry Christmas" or "Happy Holidays."

Enjoy learning English and have a wonderful holiday season!
